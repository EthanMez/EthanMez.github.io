About

I am a graduate student working on the ATLAS Experiment at CERN, affiliated with the Université de Montréal. My research focuses on using machine learning to search for physics beyond the Standard Model in proton–proton collision data collected by the ATLAS detector at the Large Hadron Collider.

I am interested in the development of novel ML techniques—including deep neural networks and anomaly detection methods—that can reveal subtle signals hidden within the enormous datasets produced at the LHC.
