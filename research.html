Research Projects

  • BumpNet — ML for Bump-Hunting
    With Jean-François Arguin · May 2024–present
    Responsible for the training paradigm of BumpNet—a supervised anomaly detection approach for histogram data—on ATLAS Monte Carlo, producing the best-performing models seen to date. Developed a method for extracting background distributions via a constrained Gaussian Process model (used by the ATLAS JMX group as a reference). Built machinery to quantify systematic uncertainties unique to BumpNet and is currently assessing sensitivity to simulated BSM signatures.
  • ATLAS Inner Tracker (ITk) Upgrade
    With Andreas Warburton & Jean-François Arguin · Jan 2023–present
    Developed the first software at Université de Montréal for analyzing defects and performance of ATLAS ITk front-ends, identifying issues unnoticed by other institutes and attracting wider input from the ATLAS ITk community. Oversees weekly visual inspections and burn-ins of hybrid panels, and designed a smoke detector with a web interface to automatically shut down burn-in procedures in case of overheating.
  • Observable Effects of Cosmic Strings
    With Robert Brandenberger · Jan–May 2024
    Simulated a 3-D model of cosmic string loops acting on galaxy distributions and compared results to observations by Peebles (2023). Found that coupled cosmic strings are a plausible explanation of observed flat structures, with effects remaining statistically significant across various observation angles.
